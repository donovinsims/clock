# Clockout — Repositioning Context & Rationale

*Why we're changing the website, the positioning, and the offer model.*

Clockout launched with a site that presented me as someone I wasn't yet. The homepage claimed we had found $127K in revenue leaks across 47 trades businesses and identified over $2.4M in leaks total. The HVAC page cited a Dallas shop that cut missed calls to zero in week one. The Roofing page cited a Florida roofer who closed 22% more inspections after we cut lead response to 47 seconds. None of it was real. I had zero clients at the time these claims went live. My core fear — and the reason I initiated this audit — was that a prospect in Rockford or Beloit would do 10 minutes of due diligence, find nothing to back any of it up, and walk away believing I was a fraud. In a 3–4 zip code market where reputation travels fast, that risk wasn't theoretical.

The second problem was inconsistency. Different pages offered different CTAs — "Book the Audit," "Book Free Audit," and "Book a 20-Minute Audit" were all live simultaneously. The guarantee changed depending on which page you landed on. Some pages were operating on the old model (free audit entry point) while others reflected the newer $497 paid audit model. The site read like two different businesses that hadn't been reconciled into one. That kind of inconsistency doesn't just confuse visitors — it signals that nobody is minding the store.

The strategic tension underneath all of it was real: I know I'm highly skilled at what I offer, and I wanted the site to reflect that confidence. The instinct wasn't wrong — it was just expressed through fabricated results instead of legitimate credentials. The fix wasn't to dial back the confidence. It was to anchor it honestly. I spent years in Product Operations at Uber managing systems at live event scale, and in digital product work at Walgreens pushing tools into a national retail operation. Those credentials are directly transferable to the operational leak problems I'm solving for local service businesses — and they're more credible than invented case studies from cities I've never worked in.

The repositioning is built on three moves: lead with the real background as proof of methodology, use a beta pricing structure ($497 all-in for audit and full implementation) to get paid while building the first real case studies, and unify the site so that every page tells the same story with the same CTA, the same guarantee, and the same honest framing. The goal is a site that can close the first client without requiring anyone to take anything on faith that isn't backed up somewhere.
