## Part 1: The Agreed Foundation

### What We're Working With

You have real credentials, zero client results, and a live site with fabricated numbers on it.
The fabricated numbers are the only actual emergency. Everything else is execution.

The strategy is to lead with methodology credibility — not invented results — and use a beta structure to get paid for the first three engagements while building the proof you need to run the full-price model.

### The Three Proof Levers (In Order)

**Lever 1 — Your background (primary, use aggressively):**
You ran Product Operations at Uber for major sports, music, and festival events across the US and Canada. Volume events where a small process failure becomes a five-figure problem in real time. You pushed digital products into national retail scale at Walgreens. These aren't soft credentials — they're exactly the operational context that makes you credible on systems, leaks, and recovery. Translated for an HVAC owner in Machesney Park, that reads as: *"I've stress-tested the exact failure modes your shop deals with at a scale 100x larger than your operation. I know where your leaks are before I walk in the door."*

That's not arrogance — it's an honest statement of transferable expertise. Deploy it.

**Lever 2 — Beta positioning (secondary, first 3 clients):**
First three clients pay $497 flat — audit and full implementation included. Normal pricing after beta is $1,494 ($497 audit + $997 implementation). They get the system at 67% off. You get documented before/after results and a publishable testimonial. Everyone wins. This is not free work. It's a strategic price with a clear exchange and a clear reason.

**Lever 3 — Free 20-minute audit (fallback, if needed):**
If beta spots aren't filling fast enough through outreach, offer a free 20-minute Revenue Leak Audit as the entry point. Still premium framing — still call it an audit, not a call. If the audit turns up $5K+, the conversation continues. If it doesn't, you've spent 20 minutes. This is the model Corey Ganim runs and it works, but it works better once you have a few case studies behind it.

### What the Site Is Doing in This Phase

The site is no longer pretending to have a track record.
It's saying: *"I know exactly what I'm doing. Here's why. Here's what the first three clients get. Here's what happens after."*

That's a harder message to fake and a harder message to doubt.

## Part 2: Unified Offer Model

### The Beta Tier (First 3 Clients Only)

| | Beta | Standard |
|---|---|---|
| Audit | Included | $497 |
| Implementation | Included | $997 |
| **Total** | **$497** | **$1,494** |
| Guarantee | 10 hrs/week back in 30 days | Same |
| In exchange for | Case study + testimonial | Nothing extra |

The $497 beta price is the same number as the standard audit alone. That anchor matters. When someone sees "$497 normally gets you just the audit — beta clients get audit plus implementation for $497," the math speaks for itself.

### The Standard Tier (Post-Beta)

- Front-end: Revenue Leak Audit at $497 — 48-hour written report, full workflow trace, leaks quantified in dollars
- Back-end: Implementation at $997 — 7-day build, full handover, you own the system outright
- Total path: $1,494

### The Guarantee (One Version, Used Everywhere)

**Kill the two-guarantee problem. Use this one everywhere:**

> "If the build doesn't recover at least 10 hours a week within 30 days, I keep working until it does. No charge."

Why this one over the $10K refund:
- A client can verify "did I get 10 hours back" in real time. They cannot verify "would we have found $10K in leaks."
- The time-back guarantee is what a trades owner actually cares about.
- It demonstrates confidence in the implementation, not just the audit.
- It's something you can actually deliver on and prove.

The "$10K in leaks or refund" guarantee can live specifically on the audit offer block. But the overarching guarantee — the one on the About page, the homepage, and every service page — should be the time-back version.

### CTA Strategy

**During beta phase:** One primary CTA across the entire site:

> "Claim a Beta Spot →"

Subtext below the CTA: `3 spots available · $497 all-in · audit + full build`

**After beta spots are filled:** Transition to:

> "Book the Audit →"

Subtext: `$497 · 48-hour report · $1,494 path if you proceed`

**One CTA. One version. Every page.** The inconsistency between "Book the audit," "Book free audit," and "Book a 20-minute audit" ends now.
